This asana comes from trikona meaning triangle and asana meaning pose. It resembles a triangle. It is also known for stretching the muscles as well as improving the bodily functions. Unlike other asanas, it needs you to keep the eyes open when you practice it in order to maintain balance.

It is a combination of many essential elements. It gives strength and stability to the legs as well as feet along with the expansion of the torso which stimulates the growth of height of the body. It stretches out arms and legs which create evenness in the body. As the practitioner balances the legs, torso and arms, the mind also becomes steady and even.

Benefits

It offers strength to back along with lower back of the body which is essential for height growth.

It leads to the strengthening of ankles, knees, chest, legs and arms.

It stretches and opens up the hips, groins, calves and hamstrings completely, along with the chest, shoulders and spine.

It increases mental and physical stability.

It improves the digestive system and stimulates all of the abdominal organs.

It assists in reducing back pain as well as sciatica. It also helps in therapy for osteoporosis, flat feet, infertility and neck pain

It helps in reducing stress and also cures anxiety.
