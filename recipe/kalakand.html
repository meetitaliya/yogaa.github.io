Kalakand Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios)
  • Silver vark (optional, for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat milk in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. Bring it to a boil.
  2. Add lemon juice to the boiling milk to curdle it. Stir gently until the milk curdles completely.
  3. Once the milk has curdled and the whey separates, remove from heat and strain the mixture through a muslin cloth to collect the chenna (curdled milk solids).
  4. Hang the chenna in the cloth to drain excess whey for about 30 minutes.
  5. Transfer the drained chenna to a pan and cook on low heat, stirring continuously to avoid sticking to the bottom.
  6. Add sugar and continue cooking until the mixture thickens and leaves the sides of the pan.
  7. Stir in cardamom powder and chopped nuts.
  8. Transfer the mixture to a greased plate and spread it evenly.
  9. Allow the Kalakand to cool down and set. If desired, apply silver vark on top for garnish.
  10. Cut the Kalakand into squares or diamond shapes and enjoy this delightful Indian sweet!

Kalakand is a popular dessert often made during festivals and celebrations.
