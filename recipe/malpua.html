Malpua Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fennel seeds (saunf)
  • A pinch of cardamom powder
  • Chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios) for garnish
  • Ghee or oil (for frying)

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, milk, and water to make a smooth batter. The batter should be slightly thicker than pancake batter.
  2. Add fennel seeds and cardamom powder to the batter. Mix well and let the batter rest for about 30 minutes.
  3. In a deep frying pan, heat ghee or oil over medium heat.
  4. Once the oil is hot, pour a ladleful of batter into the hot oil to form a round shape. You can make multiple malpuas at a time, depending on the size of your pan.
  5. Fry the malpuas until they are golden brown and crispy on both sides. Make sure to cook them on medium-low heat to ensure they are cooked through.
  6. Remove the malpuas from the oil and drain excess oil by placing them on paper towels.
  7. In a separate pan, prepare sugar syrup by dissolving sugar in a little water and bringing it to a boil. Simmer until it reaches a slightly sticky consistency.
  8. Dip the fried malpuas in the sugar syrup for a few seconds to coat them with the syrup.
  9. Garnish the malpuas with chopped nuts.
  10. Your delicious Malpuas are ready to be enjoyed as a sweet treat!

Malpua is a popular Indian dessert often made during festivals and special occasions.
