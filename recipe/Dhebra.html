Dhebra Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups bajra flour (pearl millet flour)
  • 1/4 cup grated bottle gourd
  • 1/4 cup grated carrot
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon green chili paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon ajwain (carom seeds)
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for shallow frying

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine bajra flour, grated bottle gourd, grated carrot, yogurt, ginger paste, green chili paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin seeds, ajwain, asafoetida, 1 tablespoon oil, and salt.
  2. Add water gradually and knead the mixture into a firm dough. The dough should be smooth and non-sticky.
  3. Divide the dough into small portions and shape them into round balls.
  4. Flatten each ball and use your fingers to press the center to create an indentation.
  5. Heat a griddle or tawa over medium heat and lightly grease it with oil.
  6. Place the flattened dhebra on the griddle and cook on both sides until they turn golden brown and cooked through.
  7. Remove the cooked dhebra from the griddle and serve hot with chutney or yogurt.
  8. Your wholesome and flavorful Dhebra is ready to be enjoyed as a delicious snack or meal!

Dhebra is a traditional Gujarati dish made from bajra flour and vegetables, often served with chutney or yogurt.
