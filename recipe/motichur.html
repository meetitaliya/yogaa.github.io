Motichoor Laddu Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Chopped nuts (almonds, pistachios) for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, make a batter by combining gram flour and water. The batter should be of pouring consistency.
  2. Heat ghee in a deep frying pan or kadai over medium heat.
  3. Hold a perforated ladle (boondi ladle) over the hot ghee and pour a spoonful of batter onto it.
  4. Tap the ladle gently to let small drops of batter fall into the hot ghee, forming tiny boondis. Fry until they are golden and crispy. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain excess oil.
  5. In a separate pan, prepare sugar syrup by dissolving sugar in water and bringing it to a boil. Add cardamom powder and simmer until it reaches a slightly sticky consistency.
  6. Add the fried boondis to the sugar syrup and mix gently to coat the boondis evenly. Let them soak for a few minutes.
  7. Allow the mixture to cool down slightly. While it's still warm, shape portions of the mixture into round laddus by pressing and rolling between your palms.
  8. Garnish the laddus with chopped nuts.
  9. Let the laddus cool and set completely before storing in an airtight container.
  10. Your delicious Motichoor Laddus are ready to be enjoyed as a festive treat!

Motichoor Laddus are a popular Indian sweet often served during celebrations and special occasions.
