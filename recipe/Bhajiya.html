Bhajiya (Pakora) Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 1 medium-sized onion, thinly sliced
  • 1-2 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • A handful of fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed)
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine besan, turmeric powder, red chili powder, cumin seeds, asafoetida, and salt.
  2. Add sliced onions, chopped green chilies, ginger paste, and chopped coriander leaves. Mix well to coat the ingredients with the spices.
  3. Add water gradually and mix to form a thick batter. The batter should coat the back of a spoon.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying.
  5. Take spoonfuls of the batter and gently drop them into the hot oil to form small fritters.
  6. Fry the bhajiyas on medium heat until they turn golden brown and crispy.
  7. Remove the fried bhajiyas from the oil and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  8. Your crispy and flavorful Bhajiyas (Pakoras) are ready to be enjoyed as a popular Indian snack!

Bhajiya, also known as Pakora, is a classic Indian snack made from chickpea flour and various spices.
