Medu Vada Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • A few curry leaves, chopped
  • A pinch of asafoetida
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

  1. Wash the urad dal thoroughly and soak it in water for 3-4 hours.
  2. Drain the soaked dal and grind it to a smooth and fluffy batter using minimal water.
  3. Add cumin seeds, chopped green chilies, grated ginger, chopped curry leaves, asafoetida, and salt to the batter. Mix well.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying.
  5. Wet your hands and take a small portion of the batter. Shape it into a round ball and make a hole in the center to form the vada shape.
  6. Gently slide the shaped vada into the hot oil and fry on medium heat until they turn golden brown and crispy.
  7. Remove the fried vadas from the oil and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  8. Your crispy and delicious Medu Vadas are ready to be served with chutney and sambar!

Medu Vada is a popular South Indian snack made from urad dal and is known for its crispy exterior and soft interior.
