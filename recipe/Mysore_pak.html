Mysore Pak Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour)
  • 1 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 cup water

Instructions:

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat ghee over low heat.
  2. Add besan to the ghee and roast it on low to medium heat until it turns aromatic and changes color. Stir continuously to avoid burning.
  3. In a separate saucepan, combine sugar and water. Heat until the sugar dissolves and the mixture comes to a boil. Keep the sugar syrup warm.
  4. Add the roasted besan to the warm sugar syrup and mix well to avoid lumps.
  5. Place the pan back on low heat and start adding ghee, a little at a time, while continuously stirring.
  6. Keep adding ghee and stirring until the mixture starts leaving the sides of the pan and becomes frothy.
  7. Grease a plate or tray with ghee and set it aside.
  8. Once the mixture reaches the desired consistency, pour it into the greased plate and spread it evenly.
  9. Allow it to cool down for a few minutes, and while it's still warm, cut it into diamond-shaped pieces.
  10. Let the Mysore Pak cool completely before removing the pieces from the plate.
  11. Your delicious Mysore Pak is ready to be enjoyed as a classic South Indian sweet!

Mysore Pak is a traditional South Indian sweet made from besan (gram flour), ghee, and sugar.
