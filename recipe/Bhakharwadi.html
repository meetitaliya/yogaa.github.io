Bhakharwadi Recipe

Ingredients:

  • For Outer Dough:
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 1 tablespoon semolina (sooji)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed)
  • For Filling:
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 1/4 cup roasted sesame seeds
  • 1/4 cup roasted poppy seeds
  • 1/4 cup roasted and crushed peanuts
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon tamarind pulp
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, semolina, turmeric powder, red chili powder, asafoetida, oil, and salt for the outer dough.
  2. Add water gradually and knead the mixture into a firm dough. Cover and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix all the ingredients for the filling to make a spicy and tangy mixture.
  4. Divide the dough and filling into equal portions.
  5. Roll out a portion of the dough into a thin rectangular sheet.
  6. Spread a portion of the filling evenly over the rolled dough.
  7. Roll up the dough with the filling tightly to form a cylindrical roll.
  8. Seal the edges and slice the roll into small rounds.
  9. Heat oil in a deep pan for frying.
  10. Fry the sliced rolls on medium heat until they turn golden brown and crispy.
  11. Remove the fried Bhakharwadi from the oil and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  12. Your delicious and spicy Bhakharwadi is ready to be enjoyed as a savory snack!

Bhakharwadi is a popular Maharashtrian snack made from a spiced filling rolled in a crispy outer dough.
