Kheer Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter full-fat milk
  • 1/2 cup basmati rice
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 2 tablespoons chopped nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios)
  • 1 tablespoon raisins
  • Saffron strands (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Wash the rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes.
  2. In a heavy-bottomed pan, bring the milk to a boil. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the milk.
  3. Simmer the milk and rice on low heat, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking to the bottom.
  4. As the rice cooks, it will absorb the milk and the mixture will thicken.
  5. Add sugar and continue to cook until the kheer reaches a creamy consistency.
  6. Stir in cardamom powder, chopped nuts, and raisins. Add saffron strands soaked in a little warm milk for color and flavor.
  7. Remove from heat and let the kheer cool down to room temperature.
  8. Refrigerate the kheer for a few hours before serving, allowing the flavors to meld.
  9. Serve the chilled kheer garnished with more chopped nuts and saffron strands, if desired.

Kheer is a classic Indian dessert enjoyed on various occasions and festivals.
